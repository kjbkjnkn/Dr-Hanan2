Module 05

Consumer Behaviour Influences

Understanding the "Why"

Consumer behaviour is never random. It is shaped by a complex interplay of internal and external forces that dictate how messages are received and processed.

Core Influencing Factors

Cultural

  • Core Shared Values
  • Subculture Beliefs
  • Social Class Status

Social

  • Reference Groups
  • Family Influence
  • Opinion Leaders

Personal

  • Life-cycle Stage
  • Lifestyle (AIO)
  • Economic Situation

Psych.

  • Motivation (Maslow)
  • Selective Perception
  • Learning & Memory

Strategic Consumer Insight

An insight is the "Aha!" moment where you understand the underlying emotional truth behind a consumer's action.

Insight Example:
Consumers don't just buy a gym membership for health; they buy it for the social validation and the feeling of belonging to a "driven" community.
