Module 01 • Marketing Foundations

The 4 Ps & Relationship with IMC

Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, students should be able to:

  • Understand the concept of the marketing mix (4 Ps).
  • Explain how each P supports Integrated Marketing Communications.
  • Analyze real brand examples using IMC consistency.
  • Discuss why message integration is critical for brand success.

Introduction

The marketing mix is a fundamental concept in marketing strategy. It consists of four key elements known as the 4 Ps: Product, Price, Place, and Promotion.

Integrated Marketing Communications (IMC) ensures that all marketing messages and tools work together in a consistent and unified way.

Image: The 4 Ps of Marketing

The 4 Ps Explained

Product

Design, quality, and branding offered to satisfy needs.

Price

Value, positioning, and competitive strategy costs.

Place

Distribution channels and availability locations.

Promotion

All activities used to inform and persuade customers.

IMC Relationship

IMC requires that all four Ps deliver a consistent message. If one element contradicts another, the brand image becomes weak or confusing.

Example:

A luxury watch brand must have a high price, exclusive store locations (Place), and elegant advertisements (Promotion) to match its premium quality (Product).

Class Discussion

  • Can a brand have strong promotion but weak IMC? Why?
  • How does price communicate brand positioning?
  • Give an example of a brand where the 4 Ps are not aligned.
