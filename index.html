Session 1

The 4 Ps & IMC

Foundations of marketing mix and integrated communication principles

Marketing Mix: The 4 Ps

The marketing mix represents the tactical toolkit that marketers use to implement their strategy and achieve their objectives in the target market.

Product

The goods or services offered to satisfy customer needs. This includes design, features, quality, branding, and packaging.

Price

The amount customers pay for the product. Pricing strategies must consider costs, competition, perceived value, and market positioning.

Place

Distribution channels and locations where products are available. This encompasses logistics, inventory management, and retail strategy.

Promotion

Communication strategies to inform, persuade, and remind customers. Includes advertising, PR, sales promotion, and personal selling.

Integrated Marketing Communications (IMC)

IMC is a strategic approach that coordinates all promotional activities to deliver a consistent, unified message across all channels and touchpoints.

Session 2

Situation Analysis

SWOT, PESTEL, and environmental scanning techniques

Environmental Scanning

Situation analysis involves systematically examining internal and external factors that impact marketing strategy.

SWOT Analysis

A framework for identifying Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats to inform strategic planning.

PESTEL Analysis

Examines macro-environmental factors: Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Environmental, and Legal influences on the business.

Session 3

Consumer Behaviour

The internal psychological processes of consumer decision making

Understanding Consumer Psychology

Consumer behaviour examines how individuals make decisions about purchasing, using, and disposing of products and services.

Decision-Making Process

The consumer journey typically involves: problem recognition, information search, evaluation of alternatives, purchase decision, and post-purchase behaviour.

Psychological Factors

Internal influences including motivation, perception, learning, attitudes, and personality shape consumer choices.

Session 4

Consumer Behaviour Influences

Social, cultural, and environmental impacts on the buyer

External Influences on Consumer Behaviour

Beyond internal psychology, consumers are shaped by their social context and environment.

Cultural Factors

Culture, subculture, and social class significantly influence values, preferences, and consumption patterns.

Social Factors

Reference groups, family, and social roles impact purchasing decisions and brand preferences.

Situational Factors

Physical surroundings, time pressure, and purchase context affect consumer choices.

Session 5

Communication Objectives

Setting SMART goals and establishing campaign KPIs

Strategic Goal Setting

Effective marketing communications begin with clear, measurable objectives that align with business goals.

SMART Objectives

Goals should be Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound to ensure accountability and success.

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

Metrics that track progress toward objectives, including reach, engagement, conversion, and brand awareness measures.

Communication Hierarchy

Objectives typically progress through awareness, knowledge, liking, preference, conviction, and purchase stages.

Session 6

Brand Positioning

Perceptual mapping and competitive differentiation strategy

Strategic Brand Positioning

Positioning defines how a brand occupies a distinctive place in the minds of target customers relative to competitors.

Perceptual Mapping

Visual tools that plot brands on key attribute dimensions to identify competitive space and positioning opportunities.

Differentiation Strategies

Creating unique value propositions based on product features, quality, service, innovation, or emotional benefits.

Positioning Statement

A concise declaration that articulates the target audience, frame of reference, point of difference, and reason to believe.

Session 7

Digital Word of Mouth

Electronic Word of Mouth and influencer marketing dynamics

The Power of eWOM

Electronic word of mouth represents consumer-to-consumer communication through digital channels, amplifying traditional word of mouth at unprecedented scale.

Social Influence

Online reviews, social media posts, and user-generated content significantly impact brand perception and purchase decisions.

Influencer Marketing

Partnering with content creators who have established credibility and audiences to amplify brand messages authentically.

Managing Online Reputation

Strategies for monitoring, responding to, and cultivating positive digital conversations about brands.

Brand Assignment

Project guidelines for practical brand evaluation

Course Project Overview

Apply the frameworks and concepts learned throughout the course to analyze a real brand's marketing and communication strategies.

Project Components

Your analysis should include situation analysis, consumer insights, positioning strategy, communication objectives, and tactical recommendations.

Deliverables

Submit a comprehensive written report and prepare a presentation that demonstrates strategic thinking and practical application of course concepts.

Evaluation Criteria

Projects will be assessed on analytical depth, strategic insight, creativity, practical feasibility, and professional presentation quality.
