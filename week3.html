Module 03

Consumer Behaviour

What is Consumer Behaviour?

Consumer behaviour is the study of individuals, groups, or organizations and all the activities associated with the purchase, use, and disposal of goods and services.

Academic Insight: Understanding these patterns allows marketers to align IMC tools with the internal psychological needs of the customer.

Decision-Making Process

The consumer journey follows five distinct stages:

1
Problem Recognition: Realizing a gap between current state and desired state.
2
Information Search: Seeking value through internal memory or external sources (ads, friends).
3
Evaluation of Alternatives: Comparing brands based on objective and subjective criteria.
4
Purchase Decision: Selecting the brand and making the transaction.
5
Post-Purchase Behaviour: Evaluating satisfaction; dealing with cognitive dissonance.

Influencing Factors

Decisions are rarely made in a vacuum. They are shaped by:

  • Cultural: Values, sub-cultures, and social classes.
  • Social: Reference groups, family roles, and social status.
  • Psychological: Motivation, perception, and learning.
